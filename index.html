Me

I'm Krista, a UX Designer with passion for problem solving, product strategy and designing clear, elegant solutions that are useful, engaging and satisfying.

My inspiration

I've always loved simple things. As a child I was compelled by even numbers, symmetry, balance. Growing up quickly taught me that the world is complicated and that savoring straight forward, simple outcomes was intensely appealing. I have a keen eye for clarity -- here are a few inspiring examples that highlight, for me, the beautiful nuances of complexity made simple:
  • Marriage and friendship…enjoying life's pleasures together.
  • Parenting…just enjoying my children when I slow down and savor the moment.
  • Travel…the Dolmabahce Palace in Instanbul is spectacularly extravagant yet perfectly symmetrical in every detail creating grandeur that is harmonious.
  • Cooking…the subtle yet profound nuance of layered and supporting flavors that magically balance richness, depth of flavor, acidity and sweetness.
  • Wine…this one is so obvious.
  • Yoga…the quietness of mind and spirit when singly focused on the present.
  • Good books…getting lost with compelling characters and interesting stories.
  • Beautiful things…English gardens, brilliant textiles, spices, the ocean, the mountains, sunsets, kindness

I love the complexity of making the complicated, simple.


How the pieces came together

Blessed as a mom of two and wife of a supporting husband, I’ve had lots of opportunities to balance personal growth, community engagement and professional development. In my work life I've been an effective business analyst and IT project manager and I've enjoyed that work. However, I wanted to take a deep dive into what I’ve enjoyed most: structurally and elegantly solving complex digital product design problems. Except, I didn't really have the vocabulary to articulate melding my experience with my desired focus. Then I learned about UX and thought "this is the framework to pull it all together". So, I dove head first into the 1,000+ hour UX Design course at Bloc where I've honed my UX/UI design skills while collaborating with my mentor Morgan Carter, Product Deisgner.

    WIREFRAME

    Wireframes are a tool to plan and architect an interface's structure and key functionality as well as a communication tool to inform stakeholders of design/flow decisions.

    USER CENTERED DESIGN

    Understanding the intended user's motivation and behavior relating to a specific task helps to craft an interface that is natural, engaging and most importantly usable.
    Usable = easy to learn, easy to use, easy to remember, error tolerant, and subjectively pleasing.

    BALANCE

    Varied skill set and ability to support strategic direction balanced with the aptitude for details that enhance user experience so that all stakeholders are satisfied.
    Product Strategy > Design > Front End Skills

    DESIGN AND INNOVATION

    Innovative design enhances the user experience with beautifully nuanced, engaging design which increases satisfaction and longivity with bottom line impact.

    PROTOYPING / USABILITY TESTING

    Creating protypes that test design ideas and allow for interactive user testing is key to the iterative process that utlimately yields a refined product. User feedback helps detrmine where roadblocks occur and provides opportunity to make changes before finalizing.
